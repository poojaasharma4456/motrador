How Motrador Breaks Work

Learn how live sports card group breaks work, how cards are distributed, and what to expect when joining a Motrador break.

Group break basics

What is a Group Break?

A Group Break is when collectors join together to split the cost of opening hobby boxes, premium products, and exclusive sports card collections.

This allows participants to enjoy high-end products at a lower individual cost while still having the opportunity to pull rare cards, autographs, parallels, and major hits.

How Much Do Breaks Cost?

Break pricing varies depending on:

  • Product type
  • Hobby box value
  • Number of participants
  • Break format
  • Team popularity

Some breaks offer equal pricing while others vary by team demand and product value.

Who Receives the Cards?

For team breaks, cards belong to the team displayed on the card logo.

The jersey color or memorabilia color does not determine ownership.

For cards without an official team logo:

  1. Current Team
  2. Most Games Played
  3. Draft Team (if player never appeared professionally)

How Many Times Do You Randomize?

All randomizations are performed 3 times during live breaks to ensure fairness and transparency.

Types of Breaks

Pick Your Team Break

Choose the team you want and purchase that spot directly.

Random Team Break

Every participant receives a random team once the break is filled.

Pick Your Price Break

Collectors bid together to lower the total break reserve price until all spots are filled.

Serial Number Break

Participants receive a random number (0–9). Any serial numbered card ending in that number belongs to that participant.

Example

Card #24/99 belongs to the participant with #4.

Divisional Break

Participants receive a random sports division instead of a single team.

Random Hit Break

Each purchased spot guarantees one or more hits randomly assigned after the break.

Multi-team cards and randomization

Multi-Team Cards

If a card contains multiple teams or players:

  • A randomized process determines ownership
  • Teams shown on the card receive weighted entries
  • High-value cards may allow participant agreements before randomization
  • If no agreement is reached, the card is randomized fairly between eligible participants.

Randomization Process

All randomizations are performed 3 times during live breaks to ensure fairness and transparency.

Shipping & Handling
